UX Researcher:

A UX researcher’s role is to figure out the users’ wants and needs to create a logical solution for them. They gather qualitative and quantitative data about how people think, feel, and behave by doing research with real-life users. After doing so they share their insights with the product team.

What does their day-to-day life look like?

UX researchers will organize and carry out various research activities on a daily basis, including discovery interviews, concept testing, contextual inquiry, and usability testing. After analyzing the data, they will hand over the data to the UX designer, who will then apply the data to the products.

Skills Required:

  • intuitive
  • organization
  • communication
  • analytical
  • data management
  • curious
  • open-minded

UX Writer:

A UX writer is in charge of the words on a product interface. This position is extremely important because it has a significant impact on the user's experience.

What does their day-to-day life look like?

The job of a UX writer is to plan and write text in an interface such as an error message, log-in page or a welcome message. They usually collaborate with the UX researcher to properly understand the user’s needs. Some interfaces they work with are websites, apps and other digital products.

Skills Required:

  • written
  • communication
  • collaboration
  • attention to detail
  • creativeness
  • persuasiveness
  • informative
  • clarity
  • strong vocabulary

UX/UI Designer:

A UX/UI designer's job includes focusing on the visual and interactive design of a product's interface. A user interface designer is in charge of building interactive programs that improve a customer's experience with the product. The visual design is the most significant component of the UI design since it can influence users' behaviour.

What does their day-to-day life look like?

Their role involves creating logos, being in charge of colour schemes, font selection etc. They are in charge of bringing the sketch to life.

Skills Required:

  • creative
  • empathetic
  • graphic design
  • organization
  • technical
  • attention to detail
  • visual design
  • collaboration

UX Strategist:

The UX strategist’s role is more business-related. They make sure the team is working with the business goal in mind.

What does their day-to-day life look like?

They employ business tactics to make decisions on how to beat other competitors in the market, keeping in mind that the user's needs come first. They make strategic judgments on the product's direction in order to benefit both the company and the user.

Skills Required:

  • business
  • decision making
  • strategic thinking
  • analytical
  • collaboration
